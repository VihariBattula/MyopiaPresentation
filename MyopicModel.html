Pupil Size: Controls how much light enters the eye. A larger pupil lets in more light (useful in the dark), while a smaller pupil protects the eye from bright light.

Lens Focus: The eye's lens changes shape to focus on objects. It stays flat for far-away objects and gets rounder to focus on things close up. This ability weakens as we get older (which is why older adults need reading glasses).

Glaucoma: High pressure inside the eye damages the optic nerve (the cable sending images to your brain). This causes a gradual loss of side (peripheral) vision if left untreated.

Retinal Detachment: The back lining of the eye (retina) tears and pulls away from the wall. Patients often see flashes of light or a dark "curtain" falling over their vision, which is a medical emergency that requires surgery.

Diagnostic Scanner acts like an ultrasound for the eye. It reveals the internal layers—like the cornea's thickness and the retina's health. Doctors use these scans to catch problems early, such as retinal thinning or nerve damage. The colored depth map helps them visualize the parts of the eye we normally can't see!
