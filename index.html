What is Myopia? Myopia comes from Greek roots: myo meaning to close and ops meaning eye, referring to how people squint to see clearly. Myopia is Latin for short sightedness.

Structural Change

Perfect Eye: Focus on Retina

Myopic Eye: Focus in Middle

AXIAL ELONGATION (How & Why) What happens: The eyeball physically grows and stretches from front to back, turning more oval-shaped.

How it occurs: Prolonged near-work locks focusing muscles. Without natural sunlight, the eye lacks dopamine (a chemical that halts eye growth). The eye mistakenly signals itself to stretch out.

The result: Incoming light converges before hitting the retina, blurring distance. Stretched eyes cannot shrink, making prevention crucial.
So, what’s actually happening? Most people think myopia is just weak eyes, but it’s actually about axial elongation. Axial elongation means the eyeball physically grows longer than normal. In a perfect eye, the light enters through the front and hits the retina at the back perfectly. But in a myopic eye, the eyeball grows too long, causing the light to stop traveling before reaching the retina. As a result, everything in the distance looks like a blurry mess or pixelated. And once the eye stretches, it is irreversible and cannot shrink back. The best we can do is slow or stop further progression.

It's more than just glasses.

That stretching also puts tension on the retina, which can lead to serious issues like glaucoma or retinal detachment when we get older. Glaucoma is damage to the optic nerve, often from pressure in the eye, and can lead to vision loss. Retinal detachment is when the retina pulls away from the back of the eye and can cause permanent blindness if not treated quickly.

Dopamine Inhibits Growth

The Cause: Why is this happening to our generation specifically? Near work and lack of light. First, spending hours staring at close objects (like phones and laptops) causes severe digital strain. The eye gets a signal to keep growing to adjust to that close distance. Second, we aren't getting enough outdoor time. Natural sunlight triggers dopamine in the retina, which effectively helps reduce eye growth. If you’re inside all day, your eyes don't get this inhibitor, so they just keep elongating.

Fixing It: "Eye Braces"

  • Ortho-K: Sleep-in lenses
  • Atropine: Medicated drops
Standard glasses check the blur, but don't stop the eye from growing.
Fixing it: If you already have myopia, standard glasses help you see, but they don't stop the eye from growing. However, there are new tools that act like braces for your eyes: Ortho-K: These are special hard contacts you wear only while you sleep. They reshape your eye overnight so you can see perfectly during the day without glasses. Low-Dose Atropine: These are medicated eye drops used at night that tell the eye to slow down its growth.

Protect Your Future

90 Mins: Sunlight Breaks
How it helps axial elongation: Natural sunlight stimulates the retina to release dopamine. Dopamine acts as a natural inhibitor against eye growth, effectively halting or slowing down further axial elongation. Being outdoors essentially tells the eye to stop stretching.
Break Time: Limit Continuous Near Work
How effective is it? Taking regular breaks is highly effective at reducing digital eye strain. Continuous near work locks the eye's focusing muscles in a spasmed state, triggering the eyeball to elongate. Limiting this through frequent breaks interrupts that harmful growth signal.
Eye Exam: Monitor & Treat
What specific exam? Request a Comprehensive Eye Exam with a Cycloplegic Refraction and Axial Length Measurement (Biometry). A standard visual acuity test is not enough. Biometry physically measures the length of your eyeball to precisely track axial elongation.
What you can do right now: Your eyes are still developing. Due to the fact that your eyes are still developing, your eyes might be rapidly getting worse by the day. You have the power right now to protect your vision for the next 50 or so years. Put the phone down once in a while, go outside for at least 90 minutes a day, and if you're struggling to see get a checkup. It's better late than never.
